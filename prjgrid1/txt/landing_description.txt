Panim El Panim is one of only a few kosher initiatives around the world that advocate for the return of the Ten Tribes of Israel. It stands as the direct continuation and reestablishment of the previous English initiative, "Facing Each Other," led by Rabbi Ariel Cohen Alloro. While there are currently three major advocates and activists dedicated to this matter, we expect all efforts to come under the holy curation of Rabbi Yosef Edery from Chabad. In this, we voluntarily propose Rav Edery as the sole coordinator of this mission, as there is no other individual who has uniquely taken upon his shoulders the specific task of the Rebbe Zt'l—continuing his vital work until the Messiah arrives.

So, what do we aim to achieve? Panim El Panim believes with <span class="highlight-blue">emunah sheleimah</span> (complete faith) that the return of what has been deeply misunderstood as a man called "Yeshua" to the Jewish people is happening very soon. What is this "Yeshua" that the world cannot seem to understand correctly? No, it is not the physical return of a man upon whom the Christian text is based. Rather, through accessing numerous sacred sources and recovering traditions connected to the four levels of Torah study (<span class="highlight-pink">PaRDes</span>), we have unveiled that the return of "Yeshua" to the Jewish people is nothing less than the return of the Tribe of Ephraim, whose patriarch is the prophet Yehoshua bin Nun, Alav HaShalom. 

What the Sages originally encoded in the historical account of the student of Yehoshua ben Perachiah was a profound allusion: that one day, at the end of days (<span class="highlight-yellow">B'acharit HaYamim</span>), the Ten Tribes scattered among the 70 nations—and even deeply embedded among Western peoples—would literally return to Israel. They will return to settle the land, to flock to it, and to show that "many waters cannot quench love." What then was "Yeshua" truly about? It is the Tribe of Ephraim, the chief leading tribe that will cause the lost Kingdom of Samaria to come back to Israel in a completely kosher way. As the Malbim beautifully comments on Zechariah 10:7:

<blockquote>
<strong>"And they of Ephraim shall be like a mighty man..."</strong><br>
The Ten Tribes are included under the name "Ephraim." At that time, they will awaken with might to fight the war of God.<br><br>
<strong>"...and their heart shall rejoice as through wine..."</strong><br>
A joy will be awakened in their hearts, similar to the joy that is aroused by drinking wine.<br><br>
<strong>"...yea, their children shall see it, and be glad..."</strong><br>
And their children—the generation that follows that specific generation—will no longer need to wage war. Rather, they will see the salvation that I [God] brought to their fathers, and they will rejoice with a permanent, continuous joy over the salvation of the past.
</blockquote>

This is the true return of "Yeshua"—the refinement and return of the "boar of the forest" to the Jewish people. May it be speedily and in our days that the reunification of the Tribes, which comprise the body of <span class="highlight-pink">Zeir Anpin</span> through <span class="highlight-blue">Yesod d'Zeir Anpin</span>, will unify the Holy Name of HaShem by returning to Malchus Yehudah (the halakhically established Jewish people). This is in no way the return of Eisav HaRasha; Eisav HaRasha already assimilated throughout millennia within the dispersion of the House of Israel (Ephraim/Samaria), particularly around the 1st century when these tribes were scattered across the Roman Empire. The Christian text merely tells the story of Paul attempting to bring them back in a way that was not the true path, because authentic prophecy dictates that the returning tribes will ultimately serve as holy priests to the L-rd.
